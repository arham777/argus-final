What is the theory of evolution by natural selection? The theory of evolution by natural selection, proposed by Charles Darwin in the 19th century, states that species evolve over time through a process of natural selection. This process occurs when individuals within a population possess certain traits that give them a higher chance of surviving and reproducing in their environment. These advantageous traits are then passed on to future generations, leading to the gradual change and adaptation of species over time. Natural selection is driven by factors such as competition for resources, environmental changes, and genetic variation within populations. This theory is considered one of the most important and well-supported explanations for the diversity of life on Earth. Charles Darwin introduced the theory of evolution by natural selection in his book 'On the Origin of Species'.
